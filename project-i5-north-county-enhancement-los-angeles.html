I-5 NORTH COUNTY ENHANCEMENT PROJECT

The I-5 North County Enhancement Project was developed by Los Angeles Metro and Caltrans District 7 to relieve congestion and improve freight mobility along one of Southern California's most critical freeway corridors serving Burbank, Santa Clarita, and northern Los Angeles. The project introduced additional HOV lanes in both directions, major bridge improvements, new median barriers, and extensive roadway reconstruction across multiple active traffic segments. The work was delivered through several concurrent construction contracts forming a coordinated corridor-wide freeway modernization effort.

Project Details: Owner: Public agency program; Location: LOS ANGELES COUNTY, CA; Delivery Method: Project-specific delivery; Status / Completion: Program support.

Our Role: S2 Engineering provided comprehensive Quality Management and Materials Testing services across eight separate I-5 corridor projects in support of Caltrans and LA Metro. Key quality functions included:; Review and verification of concrete and asphalt mix designs for compliance with Caltrans Standard Specifications and Special Provisions; Full-time field sampling and testing of soils, aggregates, asphalt, and structural concrete; Coordination and oversight of certified materials laboratories; Verification of testing frequencies and QA documentation compliance; Plant inspection support for asphalt and concrete production; Daily inspection of paving operations, earthwork, and structural placements; QA reporting, photo documentation, and compliance tracking S2 staff supported round-the-clock construction shifts, ensuring continuous quality coverage during high-production paving and structural operations.

Services Provided: Quality Management & QA Oversight Materials Sampling & Laboratory Testing Plant Inspection Services Pavement, Concrete & Earthwork Inspection.

Added Value: By centrally coordinating laboratory results, field inspections, and quality documentation across multiple contracts, S2 ensured consistent material performance throughout the corridor. Early identification of mix variances and compaction issues prevented pavement rework and maintained aggressive construction schedules while fully meeting Caltrans quality standards.
