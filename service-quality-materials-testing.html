Quality Management & Materials Testing

S2 Engineering has supported Caltrans and local public agencies with materials sampling, testing, and quality management for more than two decades across Districts 4, 6, 7, 8, and 9. Our teams provide dependable test data and compliance reporting that supports acceptance decisions and long-term pavement performance.

We operate Caltrans and AASHTO-accredited lab programs and coordinated field teams to deliver end-to-end quality support including sampling, batch plant inspection, QC/QA verification testing, and mix design confirmation.

Quality and Testing Scope

Field Testing

  • In-place compaction and density testing for soil, aggregate base, and HMA
  • Moisture content and grading verification for embankment and backfill
  • Fresh concrete tests including slump, air, and temperature
  • Asphalt sampling, mat density checks, and plant/lot verification

Laboratory Testing

  • Concrete cylinder fabrication, curing, and compressive strength testing
  • Asphalt mix design verification, volumetrics, and binder checks
  • Aggregate gradation, durability, and quality conformance testing
  • Soil index/classification testing and engineering property evaluation

Quality Management Functions

  • QC/QA sampling and testing oversight with independent verification support
  • Daily quality tracking, non-conformance reporting, and trend monitoring
  • Batch plant and production process quality checks
  • Corrective action support for out-of-specification conditions

Standards and Documentation

  • Testing aligned with ACI, ASTM, Caltrans, and AASHTO methods
  • Lab and field documentation prepared for agency acceptance records
  • Coordination with contractor QC and owner QA/QV representatives
  • Timely reporting for quality decisions and pay quantity support

Laboratory Coverage

S2 fixed laboratory locations include Laguna Hills, Signal Hill, Santa Clarita, Visalia, and Fremont, California. Our certified staff support concrete, asphalt, soil, and aggregate programs with scalable staffing for district and corridor-level assignments.
